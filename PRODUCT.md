# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Navapan Suthon, known online as **etsuwithtea** (nickname "est") — a 21-year-old fresh
Computer Science graduate working as a Full-Stack Developer and UX/UI Designer.

The portfolio speaks to three audiences, all arriving time-poor and skeptical:

- **Recruiters / hiring managers** evaluating for a full-time role. Their job is a fast
  yes/no; the site has to make that decision easy.
- **Freelance / contract clients** assessing range and reliability — "can this person
  actually ship what I need?"
- **Fellow developers and design peers** judging craft, taste, and credibility.

Audience is **international, English-first**. Thai context (internships at TAT, EGAT,
codehard) is framed so an outside reader gets it without local knowledge. Typical context
of use: skimming, often on mobile, deciding in seconds whether to keep looking.

## Product Purpose

Prove a combination that's rare in a fresh grad: **a developer who genuinely designs, and a
designer who genuinely ships.** Seventeen projects span fullstack web apps (Travel Buddy for
TAT, EGAT transformer management, HR systems), games (BeyondHana, Farm2D, DoodleRush), and
UX/UI prototypes.

Success is when a recruiter, client, or peer leaves convinced of that balanced-hybrid
identity and takes the next step — reaches out, shortlists, or follows. The site itself is
the headline portfolio piece: it has to be the most convincing proof of craft on the page.

## Brand Personality

**Playful, energetic, human.** The voice is warm, direct, and first-person — this is "est,"
not "the candidate." Confidence comes through personality and the work itself, not through
corporate polish. The feeling on landing should be: *this person is genuinely fun to work
with, and just as clearly capable.*

## Anti-references

The one thing to never become: **overdesigned and gimmicky.** Effects for their own sake,
motion that gets in the reader's way, visual flourish with no substance behind it. The
playfulness must come from warmth, voice, and human detail — never from piling on animation
or decoration. If a flourish doesn't help the reader understand or feel something true about
the work, it's the anti-reference, and it gets cut.

## Design Principles

1. **Playful, never gimmicky.** The defining tension. Personality lives in voice, warmth,
   and human detail — not in effects. Every animation, shape, and flourish earns its place
   by clarifying or delighting; the rest is cut. When in doubt, restraint wins.
2. **The portfolio is the proof.** Practice what you preach: the site's own craft is the
   strongest piece in the showcase. Sloppiness here undermines every claim the projects make.
3. **One person, two crafts.** Present development and design as a single balanced identity,
   neither subordinate to the other. Show, don't tell — let the project range demonstrate
   the hybrid instead of asserting it.
4. **Respect the skim.** Visitors decide in seconds. Clear hierarchy and a fast read beat
   completeness; the hybrid identity should be legible before anyone scrolls or clicks.
5. **Human by default means accessible by default.** Keyboard, contrast, and reduced-motion
   aren't a compliance checkbox — they're part of being genuinely considerate of the reader.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.2 AA**. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its background, large text ≥ 3:1. Full
keyboard navigation (interactive cards are already focusable and Enter/Space-operable; keep
that invariant). Every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative — this
matters acutely given the current marquee, continuously rotating geometric shapes, and
Framer Motion layout transitions. English-first copy, with Thai project context kept legible
for an international reader.
